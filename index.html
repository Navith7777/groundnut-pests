Pests of Groundnut

The groundnut crop, also known as the peanut or monkey nut, is a legume crop that's grown for its edible seeds. It's a major oilseed crop in India and is considered the "king of oilseeds" in the country. Here are some things to know about the groundnut crop

Major Pest of Groundnut

  • Tobacco Caterpillar
  • Groundnut Leafminer
  • Red hairy caterpillar
  • Armyworms
  • Thrips
  • Jassids
  • Termites

Pest name

Pest Name Scientific Name Management
Tobacco Caterpillar Spodoptera litura Use light traps to monitor for adult moths, and pheromone traps to attract and kill male moths spray with Azadirachtin or NPV. Release Trichogramma chilonis parasitoids, if available.
Groundnut Leafminer Aproaerema modicella Use botanical pesticides like neem, talstar, azadirachta indica, garlic, RD-9 Repellin, and Alltin Spray with dimethoate, chlorpyrifos, or acephate when there are at least five larvae per plant at the seedling stage, 10 larvae per plant at the flowering stage, or 15 larvae per plant at the pod filling stage
Red hairy caterpillar Amsacta albistriga Spray insecticides on young caterpillars, such as endosulfan 4D or carbaryl 10 D. For adult caterpillars, spray fenitrothion or chlorpyriphos Apply nuclear polyhedrosis virus at 250 LE/ha
Armyworms Spodoptera frugiperda Cyantraniliprole and thiamethoxam: Mix 6 ml/kg of seed to treat seeds Azadirachtin: Spray 5 ml/l of water with 1500 ppm azadirachtin at the seedling to early whorl stage Chlorantraniliprole: Spray 200 ml/ha of 18.5 SC chlorantraniliprole at the early stage Poison baits : Mix pesticides with nontoxic food additives and apply in the whorls at the vegetative stage or broadcast in the mature crop Cultural techniques like Early planting ,Early maturing varieties ,Reduced tillage ,Natural methods Spray infested plants with a boiled mixture of plants
Thrips Scirtothrips dorsalis Set blue sticky traps Spray neem or pungam oil, neem cake Spray with 1% azadiractin Spray with verticillium lecani
Jassids Empoasca terminalis Distant Use sticky traps or light traps to monitor jassid populations Spray systemic insecticides like Dimethioate or Phosphamidon Spray 5% neem seed kernel extract at a rate of 10 liters per acre Spray 1% Azadiractin at a rate of 250 ml per acre Spray pongam oil at a rate of 6 liters per acre
Termites Odontotermes sp. Boil 2 kg of neem leaves in 10 liters of water, filter, and add 5 liters of water
